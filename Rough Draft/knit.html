Learn to Knit

The art of knitting is old and stems from all over the world. The Egyptians knit, everyone is familair with Scandinavian patterns, and the tradition has never lost it's appeal. Knitting can be a daunting task if you've never done it before, but hopefully with this guide, you'll be creating in no time!

On his channel RJ Knits, RJ Collett explores different ways of knitting, tecniques for knitters of all levels. He is a self proclaimed "author, knitter, and animator" and on his channel, he makes "videos you can wear". His videos are informative and easy to follow, and are good for anyone who wants to learn, or sharpen their kniting skills.

Beginner Knitting for Lefties

Since knitting uses both hands regularly, you might be wondering how it can be left or right handed. But despite the usage of both hands, which direction you knit in depends on which is your dominent hand. If you are left hand dominant, or simply find knitting with your right hand dominant too difficult, try with your left hand!

Patterns

Take a look at some patterns sorted by difficulty below:

Rainbow Blanket

This is a good way to begin knitting. Simple pieces that all fit together that gives you a chance to become familiar with how to change colors while still learning how to read simple patterns. You don't need to learn any special stitches in order to create this beautiful blanket.

Link: Rainbow Baby Blanket Pattern

Stunning Scrunchie

This pattern is quick and easy, and adds a bit of personality to plain hair ties. Without taking much of your yarn or time to complete, before long you too be accessorizing your hair with scrunchies of all different colors.

Link: Stunning Scrunchie Pattern

Sea Glass Scarf

This pattern is very straight forward, and does not involve complicated stitches to complete. The pattern calls for chunky yarn, which means that not only is this pattern uncomplicated, but it is also quick and easy to complete in a timely fashion. If you choose to use the yarn they suggest, you'll get this beautiful color, but this pattern can be done with any chunky or bulky yarn you like.

Link: Seaglass Scarf Pattern

Simple knit Headband

This pattern is one of the simplest patterns out there. A headband for keeping ears warm in the winter. This pattern is quick, easy to follow, and can be done with different kinds of yarn. This pattern is also a good starting point if you hope to make more intricate headbands in the future, and can be used as a baseline for learning how to create more complicated projects.

Link: Simple knit Headband Pattern

Taiga Baby Blanket

This blanket has a little more flair to it, and takes a little more time to complete. But with this pattern you will be knitting in no time. Try out the recommended material for an even closer match to the pattern given! Sticking to the yarn weight is alwyas advisable, but if you find you want a slimmer or bulkier feel try out different weights and find what works for you!

Link: Taiga Baby Blanket Pattern

Foxtail Triange Brioche Shawl

This shawl comes with an extensive pattern, so that no stitch is left up to chance. With pictures of the finished product, and recommendations for what kinds of needles and yarn you should use, you should have no trouble putting together this beautiful piece. There are descriptions of stitches as well as full patterns

Link: Foxtail Triangle Broiche Shawl Pattern

Tundra Sweater

This sweater might look intimidating, and it is not hard to see why. The beautiful detailed neckline and hem are not easy to execute, but this pattern is available for all who wish to try it. With measurements for the finished product and pictures to see what it will look like, this sweater is the perfect winter project.

Link: Tundra Sweater Pattern

Fingerless Gloves

These gloves take five needles to make, but don't let that throw you off! This pattern is friendly to those who are not as familiar with the technique. The author of the pattern has even prepared for three different sizes, just in case you need a snugger or looser fit. The pattern is sectioned off and easy to follow, making it hard to lose your place, and easy to come back to this project if you need to take a break.

Link: Fingerless Gloves Pattern

Tablet Fair Isle Tech Vest

This is a fun one! A sweater vest for your favorite tablet device. Even though many of the products associated with this particular pattern no longer seem to be available (at the time I have retrieved this pattern) the free downloadable set of instructions is still there and waiting for you!

Link: Tablet Fair Isle Tech Vest Pattern

Sleigh Ride Socks

These socks can come in any color, and with this pattern you'll have everything you need to make it happen. With an array of sizes, there's no need to worry about whether or not these socks will fit you, or their intended wearer. This design from Drops Design also comes in many different languages, so even if English is not your first language, this pattern will not be difficult.

Link: Sleigh Ride Socks Pattern

Patons Northern Afghan Motif

This Afghan pattern is beautiful, and covers many different motifs from the north, including a moose, and snowflake. The motifs are separated, so this pattern for a blanket or pillow covering does not have a specific size, as you can simply keep adding more motifs until you're satisfied. This pattern is also offered with an option to buy the yarn, but if you choose something different, there's no reason you won't end up with this beautiful design. This pattern also comes with tips for cleaning the finished product, so you don't have to fear spils or other wear.

Link: Patons Northern Afghan Motif Pattern

Run Run Rudolph

This onesie is absolutely adorable, and perfect for the toddler in your life. There are also enough sizes in case you need to make one bigger. The pattern is easy, and is available in a multitude of different languages.

Link: Run Run Rudolph Pattern

Morning Glory

This beautiful coat is truly spectacular. With buttons and many sizes, this pattern is long and complicated, but with some determination and practice, it is easy to follow. The pattern also gives tips for adding buttons, as this pattern calls for three, and gives suggestions for which ones will work best.

Link: Morning Glory Pattern
